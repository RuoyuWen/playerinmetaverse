About Me

Game Researcher & Designer exploring the intersection of technology and human experience

Background

I'm Ruoyu Wen, a passionate Game Researcher and Designer currently pursuing a PhD in Human Computer Interaction at the University of Canterbury, New Zealand. My academic journey has taken me across multiple continents and disciplines, shaping my unique perspective on interactive entertainment and player experiences.

With a Master's degree in Game Design from Uppsala University, Sweden, and a Bachelor's degree in Communication from Huazhong University of Science and Technology, China, I bridge the gap between theoretical research and practical game development. My multicultural academic background allows me to approach game design and player research from diverse cultural perspectives.

Research Focus

My research primarily focuses on immersive entertainment experiences, particularly Alternate Reality Games (ARGs) and player motivation analysis. I'm fascinated by how players engage with digital experiences that blur the boundaries between fiction and reality, and how cultural contexts shape these interactions.

I employ mixed-method research approaches, combining quantitative analytics with qualitative insights to understand player behavior. My work explores Self-Determination Theory in gaming contexts, investigates cross-cultural gaming patterns, and examines the role of AI in creative processes.

Interests & Vision

Beyond academic research, I'm deeply interested in the practical applications of game design principles in various contexts - from educational tools to cultural preservation. I believe that games and interactive experiences have the power to create meaningful connections between people and foster deeper understanding across cultural boundaries.

My vision is to contribute to a future where game design is informed by rigorous research and cultural sensitivity, creating experiences that are not only entertaining but also meaningful and inclusive for diverse global audiences.
