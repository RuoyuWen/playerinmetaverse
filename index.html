About Me

I'm Ruoyu Wen, a Game Researcher and Designer pursuing a PhD in Human Computer Interaction at the University of Canterbury, New Zealand. My research focuses on immersive entertainment experiences, particularly Alternate Reality Games (ARGs) and player motivation.

With a Master's degree in Game Design from Uppsala University and a Bachelor's in Communication from Huazhong University of Science and Technology, I bridge the gap between theoretical research and practical game development. My work explores how players engage with immersive digital experiences across different cultural contexts.

Research & Publications

Motivational Landscapes of ARG Players

A Self-Determination Theory perspective analyzing player motivations in an Alternate Reality Game developed in China. The study examines game logs, chat interactions, and survey responses to identify distinct player types based on their motivations.

DiGRA 2024 ARG Self-Determination Theory

Sketchar: Supporting Character Design and Illustration Prototyping Using Generative AI

Sketchar is a generative AI tool designed to assist designers in rapidly prototyping game characters and illustrations. By inputting natural language descriptions, users can generate visual representations, streamlining the early stages of character design.

ACM Generative AI Character Design

Designing Conversational Agents for Cultural Institutions

This study explores the integration of conversational agents, such as chatbots, within cultural institutions like museums. It examines how these agents can transform passive visits into interactive experiences by providing personalized narratives and information.

ACM Conversational AI Museums

The Role of Chatbots in Shaping Interactive Experiences

Focusing on the deployment of chatbots in museum settings, this paper evaluates their impact on visitor engagement and knowledge retention. The findings suggest that well-designed chatbots can encourage exploration and foster deeper connections with exhibition content.

ACM Chatbots User Experience

Listen to the Sword: Using Breathing and Spatial Audio for Wuxia Games

This research introduces a game that integrates real-time breathing detection and spatial audio to enhance immersion in a martial arts context. By aligning audio-visual feedback with players' physiological inputs, the game encourages mindfulness and a deeper connection to the character's journey.

ACM Spatial Audio Biometric Gaming

I Light U Up: Exploring a New Emergent Narrative Paradigm through Light and Generative AI

"I Light U Up" is an interactive installation that utilizes light as an input medium, combined with generative AI, to create emergent narratives. Participants interact with the installation through light-based gestures, which the AI interprets to generate dynamic storylines.

ACM Interactive Installation Emergent Narrative
