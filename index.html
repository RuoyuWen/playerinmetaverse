About Me

I'm Ruoyu Wen, a Game Researcher and Designer pursuing a PhD in Human Computer Interaction at the University of Canterbury, New Zealand. My research focuses on immersive entertainment experiences, particularly Alternate Reality Games (ARGs) and player motivation.

With a Master's degree in Game Design from Uppsala University and a Bachelor's in Communication from Huazhong University of Science and Technology, I bridge the gap between theoretical research and practical game development. My work explores how players engage with immersive digital experiences across different cultural contexts.

Research & Publications

Motivational Landscapes of ARG Players

A Self-Determination Theory perspective analyzing player motivations in an Alternate Reality Game developed in China. The study examines game logs, chat interactions, and survey responses to identify distinct player types based on their motivations.

DiGRA 2024 ARG Self-Determination Theory
Read Paper

Sketchar: AI-Powered Character Design

A generative AI tool for rapid prototyping of game characters and illustrations. Using natural language descriptions, designers can generate visual representations, enhancing collaboration between artists and non-artists in the creative process.

ACM Generative AI Character Design
Read Paper

Conversational Agents for Cultural Institutions

Exploring the integration of chatbots in museums and cultural institutions to transform passive visits into interactive experiences. The research examines how conversational AI can provide personalized narratives and enhance visitor engagement.

ACM Conversational AI Museums
Read Paper

Chatbots in Interactive Experiences

Evaluating the impact of chatbots on visitor engagement and knowledge retention in museum settings. The study shows that well-designed chatbots can encourage exploration and foster deeper connections with exhibition content.

ACM Chatbots User Experience
Read Paper

Listen to the Sword: Breathing & Spatial Audio

An innovative Wuxia game integrating real-time breathing detection and spatial audio to enhance immersion. By aligning audio-visual feedback with physiological inputs, the game encourages mindfulness and deeper character connection.

ACM Spatial Audio Biometric Gaming

I Light U Up: Light-Based Narrative AI

An interactive installation utilizing light as input medium combined with generative AI to create emergent narratives. Participants use light-based gestures that AI interprets to generate dynamic storylines, exploring new possibilities for narrative experiences.
